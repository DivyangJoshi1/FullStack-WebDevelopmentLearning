Semantic Elements

Semantic tags in HTML are elements that carry meaning and convey the structure of the content they enclose. They provide context and clarity to both browsers and developers about the purpose of the content. Examples include <header>, <nav>, <main>, <section>,<article>,<footer>, etc.

Non-Semantic Elements

Non-semantic tags, on the other hand, are elements that do not inherently convey any meaning about the content they enclose. They are typically used for styling or layout purposes. Examples include <div>,<span>,<b>,<i>,<font>, etc.
